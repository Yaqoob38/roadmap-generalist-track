# AI Safety Roadmap (Generalist Track)

A practical roadmap for people trying to break into AI safety as a generalist.

**Live visual version:** [roadmap.sh/r/ai-safety-uqrd2](https://roadmap.sh/r/ai-safety-uqrd2)

**Text version:** see [`roadmap.md`](./roadmap.md)

## Why this exists

Most AI safety career resources are written for researchers. If you're not going to publish papers or train models, the path is much less obvious. You hear about BlueDot, 80,000 Hours, EA Global, Apart, Pivotal, Generator, Successif,  but not in any order, and nobody tells you which ones actually matter, which are nice-to-have, and what a realistic 12–18 month sequence looks like.

This is one person's attempt to lay it out clearly. It's biased toward what worked (or seems to be working) for someone walking the generalist path right now — UK-based, MSc starting September, applied to Generator, working toward roles at orgs like BlueDot, Apollo, Kairos, AISI.

## Who this is for

- You've heard of AI safety and want to work in it
- You're not a researcher and probably won't become one
- You want a clear sequence, not a wall of links
- You'd rather see "do this, then this" than "here are 200 resources, good luck"

## How to read it

The roadmap is sequenced top to bottom. Each stage has:

- 🟠 **Orange** = the recommended option for most people
- 🟢 **Green** = a solid alternative
- ⚪ **Grey** = optional, or no particular order needed

Stages: *What is AI Safety* → *Pick a frame* → *Gateway courses* → *Choose a sub-track* → *Build evidence and visibility* → *Programmes & fellowships* → *Land a role* → *Stay engaged*.

## How to contribute

This is a working draft maintained by one person. Programmes open and close, orgs hire and freeze, links break. Help is wanted.

**Easy ways to contribute:**

- **Spot something wrong?** Open an [Issue](../../issues) — flag dead links, outdated info, missing programmes, or anything that reads as misleading
- **Suggest an addition?** Open an Issue describing what's missing and why it belongs
- **Want to edit directly?** Fork the repo, make your change in `roadmap.md`, and open a Pull Request

If you're new to GitHub: clicking the pencil icon on any file lets you edit it in the browser and submit a PR without leaving the page.

## Maintenance

The intent is to review every quarter. If you've worked at one of the orgs mentioned, gone through one of the programmes, or have direct experience to share, your input is especially valuable.
