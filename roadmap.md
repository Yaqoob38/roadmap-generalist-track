# AI Safety Roadmap — Generalist Track

A sequenced path into AI safety for people pursuing operations, programme management, fieldbuilding, communications, or policy support roles.

**Visual version:** [roadmap.sh/r/ai-safety-uqrd2](https://roadmap.sh/r/ai-safety-uqrd2)

## Legend

- 🟠 **Recommended** — start here
- 🟢 **Alternative** — solid substitute or complement
- ⚪ **Optional** — no particular order needed

## 1. What is AI Safety?

Get a working sense of the field before committing time to it.

- 🟠 *If Anyone Builds It, Everyone Dies* — Yudkowsky & Soares
- 🟢 *Kairos: the generalist talent gap*
- 🟢 *80,000 Hours problem profile on AI safety*
- ⚪ *What Failure Looks Like* — Paul Christiano

## 2. Pick a frame

You don't need to commit to a worldview, but you should know which one motivates the work you're drawn to. The frames overlap.

- ⚪ **Superintelligence x-risk** — sudden jumps, misaligned optimisers, loss of control
- ⚪ **Gradual disempowerment** — slow erosion of human agency as AI systems mediate more decisions
- ⚪ **Near-term misuse** — present-day harms, deepfakes, surveillance, biosecurity uplift

## 3. Gateway courses

Free structured introductions. Pick one. The course matters less than actually finishing it.

- 🟠 **BlueDot AGI Strategy** — short, current, applied
- 🟠 **BlueDot AI Safety Fundamentals** — the standard intro across the field
- 🟢 **AFFINE Alignment Seminar**
- ⚪ Lens Academy
- ⚪ AISafety.com self-study tracks

> Completing a BlueDot course alone puts you in roughly the top 10,000 people educated in AI safety. Finishing a fellowship puts you in the top 1,000. Use this to calibrate where you are.

## 4. Choose your sub-track

Generalist roles in AI safety mostly fall into one of these:

- ⚪ **Operations & programme management** — keeping orgs and programmes running. The most consistently underfilled category.
- ⚪ **Education & fieldbuilding** — running courses, facilitating cohorts, building pipelines for new talent
- ⚪ **Communications & marketing** — translating the field for policymakers, journalists, the public
- ⚪ **Recruiting & admissions** — sourcing and selecting talent for orgs and programmes
- ⚪ **Founder / cofounder track** — starting new orgs in obvious gaps
- ⚪ **Policy & governance ops** — supporting the people doing policy work

You can change tracks. Pick one to focus on for the next 6 months.

## 5. Build evidence and visibility

The single biggest mistake people make is staying invisible. Roles aren't won by quietly self-studying for two years. They're won by people who have a public footprint and have already been doing the work.

- 🟠 **Be in rooms** — EA Global, EAGx, AI Safety meetups, BlueDot socials
- 🟠 **Write publicly** — LessWrong, Alignment Forum, EA Forum, your own Substack. Posting your ideas is the highest-signal action available to you.
- 🟢 **Ship public projects** — open-source resources, write-ups, sprints, anything with your name on it
- 🟢 **Contribute before being asked** — volunteer at events, facilitate cohorts, help orgs you admire

Someone who's already written one thoughtful Forum post is closer to a role than someone who's only finished a course.

## 6. Programmes & fellowships

The middle layer between courses and jobs. These give you mentorship, money, and most importantly a track record.

**Recommended:**

- 🟠 **Generator Residency** — generalist-friendly, well-resourced
- 🟠 **80,000 Hours advising** — free 1:1 careers advice, takes weeks to schedule, do it early
- 🟠 **Successif career advising** — for mid-career switchers
- 🟠 **MARS V** — research-adjacent, good for testing fit

**Alternative:**

- 🟢 **Pivotal Research Fellowship**
- 🟢 **PIBBSS Summer Fellowship**
- 🟢 **ERA Fellowship (Cambridge)**
- 🟢 **Apart Lab Fellowship**

**Funding (if you have a project):**

- ⚪ BlueDot Rapid Grants
- ⚪ AI Safety Long-Term Future Fund

## 7. Land a role

Where roles actually get posted. Set up alerts on at least two of these.

- 🟠 **AISafety.com Jobs** — most comprehensive board for the field
- 🟠 **80,000 Hours Job Board** — heavily curated, broader than just AI safety
- 🟢 **EA Opportunities Board**
- 🟢 **Org careers pages** — direct, often posted before they hit aggregators

**Where the roles are:**

- Frontier labs (Anthropic, OpenAI, GDM safety teams)
- Research nonprofits (MIRI, Redwood, Apollo, METR)
- Governance & policy (AISI, GovAI, RAND, IAPS)
- Monitoring & evaluations (Apollo, METR, Apart)
